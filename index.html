DEBORAH L. ELLIS

Deb Ellis was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. She came to the Lebanon area to attend college and received her B.S. in Elementary Education from Lebanon Valley College and taught first grade in the Northern Lebanon School District for eleven years. She received her M.Ed. and Reading Specialist Certification from Millersville University. Deb spent the rest of her career teaching as Primary Reading Specialist for grades 1-3, teaching first grade, and teaching reading and language arts in a multi-age classroom of grades 1-2 in the Manheim Central School District, in Manheim, Pennsylvania, from where she retired. Deb wrote reading and language arts curricula for grades K-1 in both school districts. She is listed in several editions of “Who’s Who Among America’s Teachers” and was nominated several times for “Manheim Central Teacher of the Year” and the Disney award for creativity in the classroom.

As a retired first grade teacher and Reading Specialist, I have written many literacy songs that have been tremendously successful for my students. These songs teach the basic reading skills in an enjoyable and memorable way. This is not the typical, stiff, fill-in-the-blanks phonics or language arts book where a skill is taught and forgotten when a page is completed. With these songs, the children do not forget, as they repeat them hundreds of times during their primary years.

Students learn letter names and sounds with memory practice by repeating tongue twisters and by singing the alphabet song (with a beat) to avoid the common problem of singing, “lmnop,” as one word.

Dr. Jack Cassidy

Texas A&M University- Corpus Christi Millersville University

"A magnificent meaningful message from a lovely literate lady."

Jack Cassidy is Professor Emeritus at Texas A&M University- Corpus Christi. At that university, he was both Associate Dean of College of Education and Director of the Center for Educational Development, Evaluation and Research (CEDER). He is also Professor Emeritus at Millersville University in

Pennsylvania. He is the former President of the International Literacy Association (Formerly International Reading Association of which he was the founder) and the Texas Association of Literacy Education (TALE) and the second president of the Diamond (Delaware) State Reading Association both of which groups he helped to establish. The distinguished Service Award from TALE is now named in his honor. He is the recipient of numerous awards in the field of literacy.

Dr Cassidy has authored scores of articles, books and book chapters on literacy and gifted education, but is probably best known for his on-going yearly trends and issues which debuted in 1997 as a column in Reading Teacher (Literacy Today) and often entitled, "What's Hot, What's Not in Literacy". The columns have been widely cited as have his more-in-depth pieces that have appeared in a number of journals including, The Reading Teacher, the Journal of Adolescent and Adult Literacy and Literacy Research and Issues.

Jack has been a classroom teacher and school reading specialist at the elementary, middle, and high school levels.For six years, he was the district K-12 reading supervisor in Newark, DE, home of the International Literacy Association.
